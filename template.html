Symmetric encryption
Before the 1970s, a single key was used to encrypt as well as decrypt a message. This often meant that the sender and the receiver of the message had to possess the question about how could the sender and receiver share the secret key with each other without it being intercepted.
For example, the infamous Enigma machine that was used during the World War II used symmetric encryption.
